## OSI — 7 layers

| # | Layer | Role | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| 7 | Application | App-level protocols | HTTP, DNS, FTP |
| 6 | Presentation | Encoding / encryption | TLS, JPEG |
| 5 | Session | Session control | NetBIOS |
| 4 | Transport | Reliable delivery | TCP, UDP |
| 3 | Network | Routing | IP, ICMP |
| 2 | Data Link | Frame on link | Ethernet, MAC |
| 1 | Physical | Bits on wire | Cables, radio |

## TCP/IP — 4 layers (real-world)

1. **Link** (≈ OSI 1–2)
2. **Internet** (≈ OSI 3)
3. **Transport** (≈ OSI 4)
4. **Application** (≈ OSI 5–7)

<Callout type="tip">
Mnemonic top-down: **A**ll **P**eople **S**eem **T**o **N**eed **D**ata **P**rocessing.
</Callout>

## Typical interview questions

- "Through how many layers does a packet pass from A to B?" — Down through all 7 on A, up through all 7 on B.
- "Where does HTTPS live?" — HTTP at Application + TLS at Presentation.
